Journey Through Time

1526 - 1857

Mughal Era

Intricate designs, grand domes, and expansive gardens characterized this period's architecture.

1858 - 1947

Colonial Period

British influence brought neo-classical and Gothic revival styles to Pakistan's architectural landscape.

1947 - 1970

Early Independence

Post-independence architecture focused on creating a new national identity, blending traditional and modern elements.

1971 - 2000

Modernization Era

This period saw the rise of contemporary architecture, with a focus on functionality and urban development.

2001 - Present

Contemporary Fusion

Modern Pakistani architecture embraces sustainable design, cutting-edge technology, and a revival of traditional motifs.

Festivals

Eid Celebration

A joyous celebration marked by prayers, feasts, and family gatherings. It can refer to either Eid-ul-Fitr or Eid-ul-Adha.

Eid Milad-un-Nabi

Celebrating the birth of Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) with processions, prayers, and religious gatherings.

Defence Day

A day to honor the heroes of Pakistan's armed forces, marked with parades and military displays.

Pakistan Day

A national holiday commemorating the Lahore Resolution, celebrated with parades and patriotic events.

Independence Day

A patriotic celebration of Pakistan's independence, featuring flag-hoisting ceremonies and cultural events.

Basant

A vibrant kite-flying festival welcoming spring, painting the skies with colorful kites.
